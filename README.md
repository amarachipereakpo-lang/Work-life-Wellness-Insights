🧘‍♂️ Worklife Wellness & Occupational Health Analysis
📌 Project Overview
Does your job dictate your health? This project analyzes the intersection of professional roles and lifestyle wellness metrics. Using a dataset of ~14.4K individuals, I explored how occupation correlates with stress levels, sleep quality, and physical activity to identify high-risk roles and provide data-driven wellness recommendations.

🛠️ The Analytical Stack
Visualization Tool: Power BI

Data Modeling: DAX for cross-metric comparisons (e.g., Sleep vs. Mental Fatigue)

Key Metrics: Stress Level, Sleep Quality, Physical Activity Duration, Caffeine Intake.

🔬 Methodology & Logic
Data Normalization: Standardized wellness scores across 8 distinct occupations (Software Engineers, Doctors, Managers, etc.).

Comparative Analysis: Leveraged clustered column charts to identify if high caffeine intake correlates with lower sleep duration across specific high-stress roles.

KPI Tracking: Implemented high-level cards to track gender distribution (Total Female: 7.18K | Total Male: 7.23K) to ensure balanced demographic insights.

📊 Key Insights from the Dashboard
The "High-Stress" Leaders: Software Engineers (12.86%) and Doctors (12.79%) recorded the highest cumulative stress levels, suggesting a need for targeted mental health support in these fields.

The Manager's Sleep Paradox: While Managers reported the highest total sleep duration hours, their sleep quality did not always scale linearly, indicating potential issues with sleep consistency.

Activity vs. Occupation: Designers lead the pack in physical activity (88.97%), while Teachers significantly lagged behind (88.79%), highlighting a sedentary risk in the education sector.

Caffeine & Fatigue: Freelancers showed the highest caffeine consumption (3.09%), likely as a coping mechanism for irregular schedules and mental fatigue levels which peaked near 13.6K.

💡 Strategic Wellness Recommendations
For Corporate HR: Implement "Digital Detox" windows for Software Engineers and Students, who currently lead in daily screen time usage (~8.51%).

For Healthcare Administrators: Focus on "Sleep Hygiene" workshops for medical staff, as they maintain high mental fatigue despite moderate activity levels.

For Freelancers: Data suggests a high dependency on caffeine; shifting toward structured "Physical Activity" (like the Designer cohort) could reduce reliance on stimulants.
